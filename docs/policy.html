Four Policy Pillars

  1. Priority Areas

Focus on the 62 Complaint Gap Areas First

Implementation: - Designate Complaint Gap areas as “priority maintenance zones” - Establish 48-hour repair target (vs. current reactive approach) - Allocate 30% of maintenance budget to these underserved areas

Rationale: These communities are systematically underserved by the 311 system. They need proactive attention, not reactive response.

Expected Impact: - Reduce lighting inequality by 40% within 2 years - Restore trust in government services - Improve safety in most vulnerable neighborhoods

  1. Satellite Monitoring

Use VIIRS Data to Supplement 311 System

Implementation: - Monthly VIIRS downloads and analysis - Automated flagging of darkening areas - Integration with work order system

Rationale: Don’t rely solely on complaints. Satellite data reveals what the 311 system misses.

Expected Impact: - Identify problems before residents complain - Catch 65% more lighting issues - Enable predictive maintenance scheduling

  1. Equity Dashboard

Build Public Monitoring Dashboard

Implementation: - Web-based dashboard tracking: - Lighting equity across neighborhoods - 311 complaint rates vs. actual darkness - Repair response times by area - LED upgrade progress and equity

Rationale: Transparency creates accountability. Public monitoring ensures equitable service delivery.

Expected Impact: - Increase public trust through transparency - Enable community advocacy with data - Hold city accountable to equity metrics

  1. Predictive Maintenance

Deploy Resources to 582 Predicted Areas

Implementation: - Use Random Forest predictions to guide inspection schedules - Preventive maintenance in predicted darkening areas - Quarterly model updates with new VIIRS data

Rationale: Act before failure. 80% of Philadelphia already decliningâ€”intervene proactively.

Expected Impact: - Reduce infrastructure failures by 50% - Lower long-term maintenance costs - Prevent safety incidents in high-risk areas

Implementation Roadmap

Phase 1: Immediate Actions (Months 1-3)

Launch Priority Area Program - Identify and map 62 Complaint Gap areas - Deploy rapid response teams - Establish 48-hour repair target

Set Up Data Infrastructure - Monthly VIIRS data downloads - Integrate with existing work order system - Train maintenance staff on new protocols

Budget: $500K initial investment Staff: 2 FTE data analysts, 5 FTE maintenance technicians

Phase 2: System Integration (Months 4-9)

Build Equity Dashboard - Design public-facing web interface - Integrate VIIRS, 311, maintenance data - Launch with community feedback sessions

Implement Predictive Maintenance - Deploy Random Forest model in production - Generate quarterly maintenance schedules - Monitor intervention effectiveness

Budget: $800K development and deployment Staff: 3 FTE developers, 1 FTE project manager

Phase 3: Evaluation & Expansion (Months 10-24)

Measure Impact - Compare pre/post lighting equity metrics - Survey residents in Complaint Gap areas - Assess safety outcomes (crime, accidents)

Refine & Expand - Adjust priority criteria based on results - Expand satellite monitoring to detect LED failures - Share methodology with other cities

Budget: $300K evaluation and refinement Staff: 2 FTE evaluators, community engagement team
