The Paradox

The Darkest Areas File 76% Fewer Complaints

Our data reveals a troubling paradox:

  • The areas with the lowest VIIRS brightness (objectively darkest)
  • Have the fewest 311 complaints (76% below city average)
  • Are also the lowest-income neighborhoods

This means the 311 system systematically misses the communities that need help most.

Our Approach

Proactive, Not Reactive

This study uses VIIRS satellite data to objectively identify dark areas, bypassing the complaint system to find underserved communities directly.

Instead of waiting for residents to report problems, we use satellite imagery to:

  1. Measure actual darkness across all neighborhoods
  2. Compare with 311 complaint patterns
  3. Identify gaps where darkness exists but complaints don’t
  4. Correlate with socioeconomic vulnerability
  5. Predict which areas will darken next

The Intervention Imperative

None

These communities need PROACTIVE intervention, not reactive 311 responses

By the time low-income communities file 311 complaints: - Infrastructure has often deteriorated significantly - Public safety concerns have escalated - Trust in government services has eroded

Our satellite-based approach enables intervention BEFORE communities reach the crisis point.
